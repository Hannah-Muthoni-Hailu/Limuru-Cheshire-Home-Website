About Us

Nestled on a sprawling piece of land in a tranquil rural area near Limuru, within Kenya’s Kiambu County and under the jurisdiction of the Archdiocese of Nairobi, stands Limuru Cheshire Home. This compassionate institution provides care and support for young girls who face physical and intellectual challenges. Girls hailing from various regions across East Africa seek refuge at this institution, hoping to find solace amidst their challenges. The community-based empowerment facility hosts 60 girls, all of whom grapple with either physical disabilities or intellectual impairments.
The institution provides an empowerment program that encompasses vocational training. The girls actively participate in activities like dressmaking, cookery, knitting, and beadwork. Additionally, they receive physiotherapy and counseling. Rehabilitation services are available to support them, enabling them to live with dignity. Furthermore, the institution conducts comprehensive community outreach programs to raise awareness about these girls.
Originally established as a residence for cancer patients, the Limuru facility transformed in 1972 to become a home for girls facing physical challenges. In 1986, it came under the management of ASN (Archdiocese of Nairobi), and today, it is one of the four institutions registered under Cheshire Disability Services Kenya (CDSK).

Our Founders

Geoffrey Leonard Cheshire

Nearly five decades ago, World War II hero Geoffrey Leonard Cheshire, a distinguished veteran, founded the facility.
After the war, he dedicated himself to charitable endeavors, establishing communities for ex-service individuals. In subsequent years, he extended his efforts by constructing homes for cancer patients and people with various disabilities worldwide.
Before his passing in 1991 at the age of 74, Cheshire, who had served as an official British observer during the dropping of the atomic bomb on Nagasaki—an event aimed at ending the war in the Far East—had initiated charitable projects in 52 countries across the globe.

Cheshire Disability Services

After the facility was converted from a cancer facility to a home for disabled children, it was placed under the management of the Cheshire Disability Services.
This is an organization dedicated to disability inclusion. CDSK collaborates with a network of 41 Local Partner Organizations (POs) in Kenya. Their joint efforts focus on implementing programs in areas such as Community-Based Rehabilitation & Care, Inclusive Lifelong Education, Socio-economic Empowerment, Sexual Reproductive Health & Rights, and Institutional Development. Additionally, CDSK serves as a Strategic Partner Organization (SPO) for Liliane Fonds in Kenya

Sr. Anne is a nun under the Assumption Sisters of Nairobi who has been serving at ASN for the past 20 years having joined the nunnery at 18yrs of age. She was posted to LCH 9yrs ago and has been working hard ever since to improve the lives of the girls.

Dr.Consolata Banda - Administrator

Sr. Consolata joined ASN when she was 20 years old and was taken through University before being posted to a number of children's homes throughout Kenya. She was posted to LCH in 2004 where she has stayed ever since developing a strong, tight bond with the girls who have passed under her care.

How can I support LCH

You can consider donating to to CDSK through the donate button or directly to the LCH by contacting us using the email and phone number provided.
You can also support a child's education and welfare by directly sponsoring them. Please contact us for more information.You

Do you have other branches?

LCH is a subsidiary of the greater Cheshire Disability Services Kenya that manages several homes geared to children living with disabilities. To see which of their homes is close to your location, visit their website here.

What is the process of admitting children to your home?

If you have a child you would like to bring to the home, we recommend coming to our center with the child where we can assess the situation and provide you with a way forward. For more information on how to get to our center, contact us using the email or phone number linked below.

Do you allow visits to your home?

We welcome everyone to visit us at LCH and we value our visitors greatly. We believe that as well as improving the lives of the children in our care, it is very important that we also enable society to understand that people living with disabilities are just like us by allowing people to visit the girls at our home. If you'd like to arrange a visit, kindly contact us in advance so that we can plan a date for the event.
